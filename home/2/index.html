systems of symbols

craft is the ability to create objects within a system of symbols. craft becomes art when values are expressed through such an object, making it an art piece. there is an uncountable number of the systems of symbols. the creation if such a system is a meta art form

the quality of an art piece is limited by the lowest of two values: the skill in a craft which governs the system of symbols, and the artistic ability of the author

overclocked morality

picture this. you have a decision to make, and it is a moral one. whether you commit to this decision or not, is your choice. but it will make you feel a certain way. perhaps a little conscience for the no, or a tad of self-pride for the yes, it doesn't matter. and now you've basically decoded your morality into a simple choice between two inner emotional states

you could find yourself in a situation where you have to "force yourself to do the right thing", but should you really have to force yourself to do good? glancing that internal conflict over, it can't be great to live a life of a latent asshole. because one day they snap and aren't latent anymore

choice prompts morality which on a subconscious level results in a positive or a negative reaction. so, on the basis that you are not a psychopath or an asshole, is it really necessary to contemplate every decision? couldn't you just skip the middle part and rely solely on how it makes you feel?

i think yes. but, there's a caveat. if you lose sight of the goal, which is being morally stable, and focus on pleasing yourself, you'll soon down spiral into a egoistic asshat, worse than those who didn't make the journey. and if your morality was lacking in the first place, it will show immediately. being yourself can be fun, or excruciatingly inconvenient

suspiria

despite countless films that parody violence, there seems to be a misconception that putting blood on screen makes media scary. and i would give it a pass, if what i watched was an original drama, not a remake of a classic horror film. but it gets worse

the original had suspense, the remake spoils half of its grand reveal in the first twenty minutes. at the same time, nothing worthwhile is added to the plot, and what was, is completely uncalled for. but it gets worse

the main character of the original, a victim of circumstances who gathers enough courage to oppose the unknown, became mary sue. and the twist is that she's the most powerful being in the movie

this begs the question, why did i have to be subjected to a power fantasy starring the 50 shades of gray protagonist because of my love for classical fucking horror?

the movie features exactly two and a half interesting scenes. both of the dream sequences, and the finale. the latter being carried by thom yorke. my takeaway is the ost, the rest? a disgrace
