side-effects of affection

my normal self is derailed the moment i realise i am attracted to the person next to me. in a heartbeat gone is the peace of mind, and my thoughts spiral into the dismaying fear of the aftermath

love is honesty and acceptance that prevail against the fear of lack thereof. if you have feelings for someone, it’s natural to fear rejection; the issue is, everything you do or say will be filtered through that fear

no matter how harsh it may sound, there’re no irreplaceable people. there’re people who are excruciatingly painful to replace. but that’s life, and what doesn't kill you makes you stronger. don’t let fears taint your being and for the love everything, don’t be afraid to be yourself

ee cummings may have agreed

i once met someone who deliberately spent time changing the titles of the songs on her playlist to lowercase. it was better to look at, she said. i thought a dislike for capital letters was the most bizarre thing ever

we tend to view letterforms solely as devices that convey information despite the fact that their most prominent quality is shape, one of the basic characteristics that carry perceptual material, that which evokes emotional reaction based on widely accepted symbolism or associations

to name a few examples, pointy triangular shapes represent cunning and prowess, bulky rectangles – strength and protection, circles and elipses – care and comfort. those simple associations are widely used in media to give audience visual queues on how they should react to particular characters

when you start thinking about letters in terms of shape and feeling, each of them takes on a unique personality. in this sense, words themselves are poems, intricate compounds of emotions

the shapes, and thus the personalities of majuscules differ from those of their miniscule counterparts, and yet they represent the same letters. majuscules are the false notes in the music of written speech. the reason i avoid them is simply poetry

how to deal with unrequited love

if you think about life, or any of its events, they all resemble the basic structure of a literary plot. or rather, the literary plot is a scaled-down model of life. by scaled-down i mean that random occurrences have higher chances of happening and everything unrelated is left out-- the Chekhov's gun principle

let's take a look at the basic plot model: call to action -> trials and tribulations -> point of no return -> rising tension -> catharsis -> resolution. now let's simplify it even more, while adding the omitted continuation: cause -> experience -> reaction -> consequences

if you're anything like me, you might get your heart broken a lot on the point of confession: fell in love -> confessed -> was turned down -> felt really sad

what most articles on the internet, or perhaps even your friends and family would tell you, is that you should respect the other person's decision and try to get it off your mind. this is completely fine, except you're being told to treat the symptoms, not the cause. you're being told to forget and move on without learning anything

the cause of any complex emotional reaction is the accumulated weight of emotional investment pressing down on your shoulders because you were expecting it to be lifted. metaphorically speaking. thus: investment -> confrontation -> reaction according to this logic, the treatment you need would be the prior suppression of emotional investment. if your goal is solely not to feel sad (or, as a matter of fact, anything), don't invest yourself into it. only that would be inhuman and you can't know for a fact how things will eventually turn out

instead, find comfort in knowing that your sadness is in direct correlation with your love, while accepting that there're things that cannot be changed. feeling sad isn't the bad part, feeling sad is being human. the bad part is when you don't feel anything, the bad part is when you no longer care

enjoy the heartbreak, for misery is wasted on the miserable

people are dehumanizing

there's this notion i've had my head beaten around with on the last listening comprehension class. the gist of it was that a person's behaviour can be controlled by a mere possibility of them being watched. they also drew parallels with Orwell's 1984, which i thought was idiotic

let's assume that our subject is lawful good and his goals are something other than hiding crime evidences in a closet. the difference between the current society and the one portrayed in the book is that no one will arrest you for holding hands in public. it wasn't the constant surveillance that had people under control, it was the fear of being brainwashed and having your whole biography rewritten

still, that doesn't mean we aren't allowed to have secrets. everyone has a share of things they wouldn't want others to know. having your privacy invaded and dealing with the consequences is hardly a pleasant experience. but the point where this turns into an obsession of showing only the positive extremities of one's personality is when i begin to speak up

it's natural to want to be at one's best and strive to make a good impression, but stuffing away anything that can meddle with the act of self-presentation is practically saying yes to being stripped off of any nuances that make one human

would you really want to live in a society where every single human being is perfect? then why pretend it to be that way? if you're going to invest so much time and effort into creating an image, you might aswell work towards becoming a better person

don't let some notion be imposed on you by society. let yourself be vulnerable, but be careful who you're vulnerable around. blush every once in a while. dance a little next time you're waiting for a traffic light to change. don't box yourself in

the discomfort of conformity

let's say you're one of the 5 people participating in an experiment on perceptual judgement. you are shown 4 lines of various lengths:

a ————
b ————————
c ————————————
x ————————

you are then asked to judge which of the first three most closely matches x. the first participant inspects the lines and to your surprise says, "the line a". you might think he's blind or mad, but then one by one every other person in the room says the same thing. debating your own sanity, you hesitate before agreeing with the group. about a third does. the Asch experiment on conformity

if a movie is scoring in the high ninths on imdb, it must be really good, right? well, no. a system that gathers anonymous clicks on a single scale from users across all countries, occupations, ages, degrees of knowledge in literature and film making and et cetera cannot accurately measure how good something is. it simply shows how many people were exposed to a piece of media and how appealing they've found it. such a rating is a measurement of disposability, not quality

we tend to base our perception of reality on whatever the majority convinces us to be true. we're willing to go along with what's popular or accepted. we turn away from critical thinking in favour of not deviating from the society. we are social animals. this bothers me. like, a lot

don't let yourself completely rely on the opinion of others, especially if the judgement required is purely subjective. stand your ground until experience and factual knowledge prove you otherwise. your system of values and perception is as unique as you are. think for yourself and don't give in to conformity
