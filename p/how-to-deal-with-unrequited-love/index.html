how to deal with unrequited love

if you think about life, or any of its events, they all resemble the basic structure of a literary plot. or rather, the literary plot is a scaled-down model of life. by scaled-down i mean that random occurrences have higher chances of happening and everything unrelated is left out-- the Chekhov's gun principle

let's take a look at the basic plot model: call to action -> trials and tribulations -> point of no return -> rising tension -> catharsis -> resolution. now let's simplify it even more, while adding the omitted continuation: cause -> experience -> reaction -> consequences

if you're anything like me, you might get your heart broken a lot on the point of confession: fell in love -> confessed -> was turned down -> felt really sad

what most articles on the internet, or perhaps even your friends and family would tell you, is that you should respect the other person's decision and try to get it off your mind. this is completely fine, except you're being told to treat the symptoms, not the cause. you're being told to forget and move on without learning anything

the cause of any complex emotional reaction is the accumulated weight of emotional investment pressing down on your shoulders because you were expecting it to be lifted. metaphorically speaking. thus: investment -> confrontation -> reaction according to this logic, the treatment you need would be the prior suppression of emotional investment. if your goal is solely not to feel sad (or, as a matter of fact, anything), don't invest yourself into it. only that would be inhuman and you can't know for a fact how things will eventually turn out

instead, find comfort in knowing that your sadness is in direct correlation with your love, while accepting that there're things that cannot be changed. feeling sad isn't the bad part, feeling sad is being human. the bad part is when you don't feel anything, the bad part is when you no longer care

enjoy the heartbreak, for misery is wasted on the miserable
