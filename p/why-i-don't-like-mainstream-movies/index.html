why i don't like mainstream movies

the villain captures the hero's loved one. the hero sets off on a journey to rescue the damsel in distress. the hero recruits two stalwart knights and challenges the villain. the hero is victorious. the couple is reunited

sounds like a typical nineteenth century play, right? but it's also about two thirds of deadpool 2016. hollywood treads the beaten path of whatever's proven to work, avoiding novel plot design as if it were plague— your typical braindead member of the audience doesn't digest that very well. gags fed spaciously do much better
