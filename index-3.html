the covenant of integrity

let us set ethics aside. i like to imagine that every time a lie is told the reality splits in two: what somebody is led to believe and the actual facts. and the more we lie, the more confusing it gets. we end up frustrated, rummaging through myriads of realities in a desperate search of the one we can call true

but there're those who see no benefit in delusions or have abstained from them, the members of the covenant. the reasons are many, but their goal is one: to preserve the integrity of reality. they do not lie, believe what they're told, remember that it may not be true, and leave the rest to the speaker's conscience

love: a systematic approach

  • mania. the common. selfish, objectifying, possessive. romanticised in literature, movies, etc. in the forms of desire and passion. in essence-- a wrongful claim. the counterweight-- fear of rejection. the consequence-- jealousy, emotional dependence. sheer poison of love. negative

  • pragma. the convenient. i am alone, i seek self-contentment. i'll consent to your feeling not because you're important to me, but because i need the status. consequence-- dysfunctional relationships. doesn't last. negative

  • ludus. the playful. incidental, drunken. in the spur of the moment. leads on. consequence-- a single party suffers unless mutual. neutral

  • eros. the physical. sexual attraction. materialistic, stereotypical. hardly exists on its own. the counterweight may disrupt otherwise healthy mental relationships. consequence-- none. neutral

  • storge. the instinctual. a higher form of friendship. intimate, compassionate, devoted. love within family. long-lasting companionship. positive

  • agape. the transcendent. unconditional, genuine, ever-forgiving. love between god and man. pure and selfless acceptance of every aspect of someone's existence. knows no fear of loss. almost never exists. positive

eerie summer

a gorgeous slice of lo-fi self-pity, mellow enough to lull me into sleep during long train rides, eerie summer has been a top listen of mine for the past two years

subjective as music can be, i believe that victoria’s lyrics, in spite of their simplicity, are special in how they manage to convey ideas mostly through actions

you disappear for weeks
and i've been dreaming of ur face
and you you never text me back
but i can't think of anybody else
cuz im into u
im into u baaaaaaby

pure and raw, and not a single epithet. once you start explaining a feeling, it loses some of its depth. here, like in any proper work of literature, you aren't told how you're supposed to feel. you're free to figure it out yourself

according to the song writer, “weird around you” is inspired by the feelings daria has for trent, from the animated series of the same title. in fact, most of the songs are quite daria-esque. that's already enough to get me hooked

i feel so weird around you, i've never really been in love
i keep my words and my problems, i'd rather keep it bottled up
and even though i know i miss you, i'd rather keep it all inside
and now i know i will regret it, i'd rather keep it all inside

in a way, her music is escapist. you may be an advocate of stoicism like me, but you’re human too. you can be vulnerable. you can feel sad and helpless and wish to god for a bit of compassion and receive none

sometimes all we can do is cry. and sometimes, it's what we need
