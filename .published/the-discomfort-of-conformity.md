let's say you're one of the 5 people participating in an experiment on perceptual judgement. you are shown 4 lines of various lengths:

```
a ————
b ————————
c ————————————
x ————————
```

you are then asked to judge which of the first three most closely matches x. the first participant inspects the lines and to your surprise says, "the line a". you might think he's blind or mad, but then one by one every other person in the room says the same thing. debating your own sanity, you hesitate before agreeing with the group. about a third does. the Asch experiment on conformity

if a movie is scoring in the high ninths on imdb, it must be really good, right? well, no. a system that gathers anonymous clicks on a single scale from users across all countries, occupations, ages, degrees of knowledge in literature and film making and et cetera cannot accurately measure how good something is. it simply shows how many people were exposed to a piece of media and how appealing they've found it. such a rating is a measurement of disposability, not quality

we tend to base our perception of reality on whatever the majority convinces us to be true. we're willing to go along with what's popular or accepted. we turn away from critical thinking in favour of not deviating from the society. we are social animals. this bothers me. like, a lot

don't let yourself completely rely on the opinion of others, especially if the judgement required is purely subjective. stand your ground until experience and factual knowledge prove you otherwise. your system of values and perception is as unique as you are. think for yourself and don't give in to conformity
