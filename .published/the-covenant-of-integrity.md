let us set ethics aside. i like to imagine that every time a lie is told the reality splits in two: what somebody is led to believe and the actual facts. and the more we lie, the more confusing it gets. we end up frustrated, rummaging through myriads of realities in a desperate search of the one we can call true

but there're those who see no benefit in delusions or have abstained from them, the members of the covenant. the reasons are many, but their goal is one: to preserve the integrity of reality. they do not lie, believe what they're told, remember that it may not be true, and leave the rest to the speaker's conscience
