people are dehumanizing

there's this notion i've had my head beaten around with on the last listening comprehension class. the gist of it was that a person's behaviour can be controlled by a mere possibility of them being watched. they also drew parallels with Orwell's 1984, which i thought was idiotic

let's assume that our subject is lawful good and his goals are something other than hiding crime evidences in a closet. the difference between the current society and the one portrayed in the book is that no one will arrest you for holding hands in public. it wasn't the constant surveillance that had people under control, it was the fear of being brainwashed and having your whole biography rewritten

still, that doesn't mean we aren't allowed to have secrets. everyone has a share of things they wouldn't want others to know. having your privacy invaded and dealing with the consequences is hardly a pleasant experience. but the point where this turns into an obsession of showing only the positive extremities of one's personality is when i begin to speak up

it's natural to want to be at one's best and strive to make a good impression, but stuffing away anything that can meddle with the act of self-presentation is practically saying yes to being stripped off of any nuances that make one human

would you really want to live in a society where every single human being is perfect? then why pretend it to be that way? if you're going to invest so much time and effort into creating an image, you might aswell work towards becoming a better person

don't let some notion be imposed on you by society. let yourself be vulnerable, but be careful who you're vulnerable around. blush every once in a while. dance a little next time you're waiting for a traffic light to change. don't box yourself in
